# Persona — {Name and role}

- **Status:** draft | in review | validated | retired

## Snapshot

One paragraph elevator pitch — who they are, what they do, what they need from us.

## Background and context

Role, environment, how they got here. Specific enough to feel like a person, not a category.

## Goals

What they're trying to accomplish at three levels:

- **Life / career:** the big arc.
- **Work / project:** what they're trying to ship this quarter.
- **Right now:** what they need to get done today using a tool like ours.

## Frustrations

Concrete pain points in their current workflow. Each names the trigger, the cost, and (if known) the workaround they currently use.

## Behaviors and habits

How they actually work — observable patterns, not aspirations. "Checks email at 7am," not "is organized."

## Tools today

What they currently use to do the job we want to do better. Honest assessment of why those tools work or do not.

## Quote

A short, real-feeling statement that captures their stance. Verbatim from interviews where possible; clearly paraphrased otherwise.

## What this implies for the product

The bridge from persona to product decisions. Two to five bullets, each tied to a feature direction or a constraint.

## Open questions / what to validate

Claims in this persona that need more evidence. What would we need to learn to upgrade Status from draft to validated?
