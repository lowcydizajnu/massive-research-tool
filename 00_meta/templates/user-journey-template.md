# User journey — {Journey name}

A user journey complements a user flow: the flow is *what* happens, the journey is *how it feels* and *when* over time.

- **Persona:** {link to persona}
- **Related flow(s):** {links to user flows in 02_product/user-flows/}
- **Time horizon:** single session | days | weeks | months
- **Status:** draft | reviewed | implemented | retired

## Trigger

What event in the user's life or work begins this journey.

## Goal

What the user is trying to achieve over the whole journey, not just one session.

## Stages

For each stage of the journey:

### Stage {n} — {name}

- **Touchpoints:** the screens, emails, notifications, or external channels involved.
- **Actions:** what the user does.
- **Thoughts:** what the user is thinking (verbatim quotes from research, where possible).
- **Emotions:** what the user is feeling. Be specific — "frustrated by ambiguous error message" is better than "frustrated."
- **Pain points:** what is failing or fragile.
- **Opportunities:** what we could do better.
- **Relevant insight:** link the research insight that informs how we should design this stage.

## Moments of truth

The two or three points in the journey where the user's overall opinion of the product is decided. Design effort concentrates here.

## Outcome

What the user leaves with — emotionally, practically, in terms of what they tell others.

## Diagram

Embed or link the journey map (a timeline with emotion axis is the most common form).
